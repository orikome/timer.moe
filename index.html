FAQ

Do timers keep running when I close the tab?

Yes. timer.moe records timestamps rather than counting in real time, so when you return the timer shows the correct elapsed time, as if it never stopped. This works even after closing the browser, as long as the timer was saved to localStorage.

Why is timer.moe good for ADHD?

ADHD affects time perception and task switching. timer.moe supports time-boxing, lets you monitor multiple tasks without stopping and restarting a single timer, and uses colour-coded cards so you can instantly see which timer belongs to which task. Background tracking means you never lose progress after switching tabs.

How do I use the Pomodoro technique with timer.moe?

Set a timer to Countdown mode and enter 25 minutes. Press Start and focus on one task. When it finishes, add a second timer for your 5-minute break. timer.moe lets you run both side by side so you always know your work time and break time.

Can I sync timers across devices?

Yes. Sign in with a Google account or email address and your timers sync automatically to Firebase Firestore. Without an account, timers are stored only in your browser's localStorage.

Is timer.moe free?

Completely free. No subscription, no ads, no paywalled features. Everything works without creating an account.

Does timer.moe work on mobile?

Yes. The interface is fully responsive and works in any modern mobile browser. You can install timer.moe as a PWA on Android or iOS. Use your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option for quick access from your home screen.
